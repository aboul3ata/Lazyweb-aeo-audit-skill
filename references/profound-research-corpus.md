## Audit Implications

- Test multiple prompt phrasings. Query language can reshape citations, so one brand query is not enough.
- Evaluate third-party citation surfaces. LinkedIn, Reddit, YouTube, review sites, and category pages may influence AI answers as much as owned pages.
- Distinguish shopping/commercial queries from informational queries. Commerce triggers and product feeds matter for ecommerce sites.
- Monitor volatility over time. AI search results shift, so report snapshots should include date and validation prompts.
- Measure branded and non-branded discovery separately. Zero-click and branded-link changes can mask whether a site is actually cited.
- Check entity clarity. Entity updates and fewer mentions mean ambiguous brands need stronger schema, sameAs, and third-party anchors.
- Include platform variance. ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Google AI sources can overlap differently.
- Preserve classic SEO fundamentals. Ranking positions, crawlability, and Google index alignment still affect AI citations.

## Internal Article Takeaways From Browse Pass

The following takeaways come from fetching the internal Profound article/report URLs, reading titles, descriptions, and on-page text snippets. They are intentionally paraphrased and should be treated as audit heuristics, not copied article text.

- `chatgpt-referrals-branded-links`: ChatGPT can change referral behavior overnight; homepage links and branded links can materially change zero-click assumptions. Audits should check whether the homepage is a strong citation/referral target.
- `how-query-language-reshapes-ai-citations`: Query language and market language change citation patterns. Global sites need multilingual prompt testing and localized authority checks, not only English brand prompts.
- `chatgpt-retail-target-walmart`: AI shopping results are unstable and retailer presence differs by rank versus total carousel presence. Ecommerce audits should separate product-card eligibility, rank-1 buy links, and repeated-run stability.
- `chatgpt-shopping-prediction`: Shopping cards are driven more by concrete shippable product nouns than generic purchase intent language. Ecommerce pages need extractable product category, constraints, price, and feature data.
- `linkedin-is-the-most-cited-domain-for-professional-queries-in-ai-search`: LinkedIn can be a major citation surface for professional queries. B2B audits should evaluate company/person profiles and social authority, not just owned webpages.
- `chatgpt-shopping-analysis`: Shopping triggers are sparse and persistent only for certain prompt/category patterns. Reports should identify whether the site is in a category where shopping surfaces matter.
- `chatgpt-reddit-youtube-citations`: Social citations differ by platform; Reddit threads, YouTube channels, LinkedIn profiles/posts, and Instagram surfaces behave differently. Audits should map which social source types are plausible for the category.
- `the-data-on-reddit-and-ai-search`: Human discussion can act as evidence for answer engines. Sites with no community or UGC footprint may need credible third-party conversations, reviews, or forum presence.
- `chatgpt-entity-update`: Brand/entity recognition can reduce the number of surfaced brands. Ambiguous or weakly structured brands need stronger entity schema, sameAs anchors, and offsite corroboration.
- `ai-search-shift`: Classic search indexes still influence AI citations. AEO audits should not skip crawlability, indexability, rankings, canonicals, and source-page quality.
- `ai-search-volatility`: Citation drift is normal. Audits should be dated snapshots and recommend recurring measurement for important prompts.
- `citation-overlap-strategy`: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Copilot can cite different source sets. Visibility work must be platform-specific.
- `chatgpt-intent-landmark-study`: Many ChatGPT prompts are generative or action-oriented rather than classic search queries. AEO content should support tasks, creation, comparison, and decision prompts.
- `ai-platform-citation-patterns`: AI platforms have distinct source preferences. Reports should mention platform variance and not overgeneralize from one answer engine.
- `ai-shopping-2025`: Answer engines are becoming shopping decision surfaces. Commercial sites should expose product, pricing, availability, reviews, returns, and buying paths in structured, crawlable forms.
- `the-machine-customer-era`: Machine customers form perspectives from multiple answer engines and sources. Brands need machine-readable facts plus trusted external corroboration.
